Solution

We first solve the expectation (mean) of the empirical mean and variance of yi. The empirical mean of yi is given by

(1)μ^=1n∑i=1nyi

The expectation of μ^ is

(2)E(μ^)=E(1n∑i=1nyi)(3)=1n∑i=1nE(yi)(4)=E(yi)

Hence, μ^ is an unbiased estimator of the expectation of the random variable yi.

The empirical variance of yi is given by

(5)σ^2=1n∑i=1n(yi−μ^)2

The expectation of σ^2 is

(6)E(σ^2)=nn−1Var(yi)

Since we are working in the large n limit,

(7)limn→∞nn−1=1

We have

(8)∀ ϵ>0, limn→∞P[|σ^2−Var(yi)|<ϵ]=1

Hence, σ^2 is a consistent estimator of the variance of the random variable yi.

We now consider the linear transformation and try solving the expectation and variance of the random variable yi. The linear transformation is given by

If we strictly follow this line of computations, we will have to compute the fourth moment of the random variables, which will involve many many terms as the question stated. If we use approximation and treat yi as a Gaussian random variable

(41)yi∼N(E(yi),Var(yi))

then the variance of the sample variance of Gaussian random variables obeys chi-square distribution.

(42)1Var(yi)∑i=1n(yi−μ^)2∼χn−12

(43)Var[∑i=1n(yi−μ^)2]=[Var(yi)]2⋅2(n−1)
